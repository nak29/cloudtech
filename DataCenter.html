Data Centers


A data center is a facility that centralizes an organization’s shared IT operations and equipment. A data center is used for many different tasks, such as storing, processing, and disseminating data and applications across an entire system. Reliability and security are must-have traits of data centers, as if anyone could bust into them, they would not be able to hold such classified information all in one place.

Before the early 2010’s, data centers were physical infrastructures, but now that technology has greatly advanced, virtualized infrastructures have become much more popular. Society now possesses the power to constantly upload and download data via the cloud, and with over 7 million of them worldwide, they have greatly benefited from it. Because of data centers being mostly cloud-based, they need even more security checks than ever before. Most of these data centers are for private companies, but even enterprises such as Amazon and Google need data centers. Regardless, these data centers hold large magnitudes of data that should not ever be breached.
